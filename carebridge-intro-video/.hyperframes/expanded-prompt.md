# CareBridge AI — 9:16 Product Introduction

## Style block

Portrait 1080×1920, approximately 150 seconds. Warm Apple-native care technology. Use only the CareBridge palette from `DESIGN.md`: canvas `#F7FAF6`, paper `#FFFFFF`, ink `#183326`, primary green `#1F8F57`, soft green `#E0F5E8`, deep green `#0D6840`, warning `#F2B833`, danger `#E64038`, muted `#557064`, hairline `#C8DCCE`. Typography is Noto Sans JP plus Space Mono.

## Rhythm

quiet-hook → tension-build → PROBLEM-PEAK → bridge-reveal → product-proof → feature-cascade → practical-breathe → TECHNICAL-CLIMAX → human-close

## Global direction

- A thin “handoff line” recurs through the film, initially broken and later continuous.
- Every scene has background texture, midground message, and foreground metadata.
- Primary transitions are 0.7s blur crossfades. Major section changes use 0.55s vertical pushes.
- App captures live inside large rounded phone frames with slow 2–4% camera drift.
- Narration is calm Traditional Chinese. On-screen captions use short phrases, not full paragraphs.

## Scene plan

### 1. Hook — 0:00–0:12

Concept: A single care update tries to cross a broken line between two people. The question appears: when care changes hands, what gets lost?

Depth: soft green glow and paper grain in BG; broken handoff line and two human nodes in MG; timecode, “CARE HANDOFF / 01”, and registration marks in FG.

Choreography: line DRAWS then fractures; question FLOATS upward; keyword “遺漏” LOCKS IN red.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.75s.

### 2. Pain point — language gap — 0:12–0:26

Concept: Two chat bubbles in different languages nearly meet but remain offset. Translation tools and isolated words do not preserve context.

Depth: oversized ghost words in multiple languages; opposing chat bubbles; waveform, language chips, and mismatch marks.

Choreography: bubbles SLIDE from opposite edges; waveform TYPES ON; mismatch gap PULSES.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.7s.

### 3. Pain point — information gap — 0:26–0:40

Concept: LINE messages, verbal memory, notes, and tasks scatter across the frame. The handoff line cannot connect them.

Depth: drifting note fragments; central “今天發生了什麼？” statement; tiny timestamps and source labels.

Choreography: fragments CASCADE in; central question STAMPS into place; two risk counters COUNT UP.

Transition: vertical push, 0.55s, marking the move from problem to solution.

### 4. Solution reveal — 0:40–0:54

Concept: CareBridge AI gathers every fragment into one shared care timeline. The logo and home screen become the bridge.

Depth: continuous green line, logo halo, home app footage in a phone shell; “RECORD → UNDERSTAND → HANDOFF” metadata.

Choreography: fragments ASSEMBLE into the phone; logo GROWS; bridge line completes.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.75s.

### 5. Feature — AI handoff summary + status light — 0:54–1:10

Concept: Show red, yellow, and green home states, then settle on the AI-generated daily handoff summary.

Depth: three layered phone captures; status dots; AI summary highlight; status legend and on-device badge.

Choreography: phones FAN OUT; status lights PULSE once; summary card LIFTS forward.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.7s.

### 6. Feature — multimodal smart recording — 1:10–1:28

Concept: One natural sentence becomes structured food, medication, bowel, and mood records. Demonstrate text, voice, and quick status entry.

Depth: text-entry footage, voice-entry footage, quick-record screen; category chips and arrow paths.

Choreography: source sentence TYPES ON; category chips SPLIT outward; phone footage slowly PUSHES closer.

Transition: vertical push, 0.55s.

### 7. Feature — cross-language communication — 1:28–1:42

Concept: A caregiver writes in their preferred language and the family receives clear context in Chinese.

Depth: chat screenshot, two language chips, translation bridge line, speech waveform.

Choreography: original message SLIDES in; translated text REVEALS beneath; language chips SWAP positions.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.75s.

### 8. Everyday functions — 1:42–1:58

Concept: The intelligence sits inside a complete care workflow: task calendar, reminders, profile data, and QR caregiver invitation.

Depth: staggered screenshots in an editorial column; feature labels; calendar dates, notification dot, QR corner marks.

Choreography: screens PAGE upward; labels CLICK into place; QR frame DRAWS around the code.

Transition: blur crossfade, 0.7s.

### 9. Technology — 1:58–2:18

Concept: Reveal the Apple-native stack as a privacy-first architecture around the phone, with on-device AI at the center.

Depth: central phone chip; orbiting framework labels: Foundation Models, Core ML, Natural Language, Speech, Translation, SwiftUI, SwiftData + CloudKit, UserNotifications.

Choreography: central chip LOCKS IN; framework labels ASSEMBLE in sequence; “ON-DEVICE / OFFLINE / PRIVATE” bars FILL.

Transition: focus pull, 0.8s.

### 10. Closing — 2:18–2:30

Concept: Return from technology to the human purpose. A complete bridge joins family and caregiver around one clear daily picture.

Depth: logo, continuous bridge line, two human nodes, closing statement, team credit.

Choreography: line DRAWS fully; logo BREATHES; final statement settles and holds.

Final fade: 0.8s to canvas.

## Recurring motifs

- Broken-to-continuous handoff line.
- Small Space Mono metadata: scene number, timestamp, ON-DEVICE, SYNCED.
- Rounded phone frames, status dots, and subtle green-tinted shadow.

## Negative prompt

No dark cyberpunk canvas, no generic AI particles, no dense slides, no tiny technical diagrams, no invented product claims, no jump cuts, and no footage stretched outside its native portrait aspect ratio.
